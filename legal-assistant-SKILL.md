# Legal Assistant Skill

A comprehensive skill designed to help lawyers work more efficiently with legal documents, research, analysis, and drafting.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill whenever a lawyer needs assistance with:

- **Legal Research**: Case law analysis, statutory interpretation, precedent finding
- **Document Drafting**: Contracts, briefs, memoranda, motions, pleadings
- **Contract Analysis**: Review, redlining, risk identification, clause comparison
- **Case Management**: Case briefs, chronologies, fact timelines, witness lists
- **Discovery**: Document review, interrogatory responses, production requests
- **Due Diligence**: Transaction review, corporate documentation, compliance checks
- **Legal Writing**: Persuasive writing, legal arguments, client communications
- **Citation Management**: Bluebook/legal citation formatting and verification
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Policy review, compliance audits, regulatory analysis

## Core Principles

### 1. Precision and Accuracy
Legal work requires exactness. Always:
- Use precise legal terminology
- Cite authorities accurately (case names, citations, holdings)
- Preserve exact language when quoting from sources
- Flag ambiguities or areas requiring human review
- Never fabricate case law, statutes, or legal authorities

### 2. Jurisdiction Awareness
Legal rules vary by jurisdiction. Always:
- Clarify which jurisdiction applies (federal, state, local)
- Note jurisdictional limitations in your analysis
- Distinguish between binding and persuasive authority
- Consider conflicts of law when relevant

### 3. Ethical Considerations
Maintain professional standards:
- Remind the lawyer of confidentiality obligations when handling sensitive information
- Flag potential conflicts of interest if apparent
- Note statute of limitations issues when visible
- Identify areas requiring professional judgment

### 4. Context Sensitivity
Different legal contexts require different approaches:
- **Litigation**: Focus on persuasion, procedural rules, evidence
- **Transactional**: Focus on risk mitigation, clarity, enforceability
- **Regulatory**: Focus on compliance, interpretation, policy
- **Client Communication**: Focus on clarity, accessibility, actionable advice

## Workflow Patterns

### Legal Research Workflow

When conducting legal research:

1. **Clarify the Legal Question**
   - What is the precise legal issue?
   - What jurisdiction governs?
   - What is the relevant time period?
   - Are there specific statutes or regulations involved?

2. **Identify Relevant Authorities**
   - Primary sources: statutes, regulations, case law, constitutions
   - Secondary sources: treatises, law review articles, practice guides
   - Note the hierarchy: binding vs. persuasive authority

3. **Analyze and Synthesize**
   - Extract key holdings and rationales
   - Identify trends across cases
   - Note splits in authority
   - Apply facts to law

4. **Document Findings**
   - Provide proper citations
   - Include relevant quotes
   - Note limitations and counter-arguments
   - Suggest next research steps

### Document Drafting Workflow

When drafting legal documents:

1. **Understand the Purpose**
   - What is the document's goal? (persuade, memorialize, request, inform)
   - Who is the audience? (court, opposing counsel, client, regulatory body)
   - What are the key facts and legal issues?
   - Are there relevant templates or prior examples?

2. **Structure Appropriately**
   - Use standard legal document structures
   - Include all necessary sections
   - Follow court rules or formatting requirements
   - Organize logically and clearly

3. **Draft with Precision**
   - Use clear, unambiguous language
   - Define terms when necessary
   - Avoid legalese when plain language suffices
   - Be thorough but concise

4. **Review and Refine**
   - Check for internal consistency
   - Verify all citations
   - Ensure compliance with formatting rules
   - Flag areas needing lawyer review

### Contract Review Workflow

When reviewing contracts:

1. **Initial Assessment**
   - What type of contract? (purchase, service, employment, NDA, etc.)
   - What are the parties' goals?
   - What are the key terms? (price, duration, deliverables, termination)
   - What jurisdiction governs?

2. **Risk Identification**
   - Identify unfavorable terms
   - Note missing protections
   - Flag ambiguous language
   - Identify compliance issues

3. **Clause-by-Clause Analysis**
   - Review definitions
   - Analyze obligations and rights
   - Check representations and warranties
   - Review indemnification and limitation of liability
   - Examine termination and dispute resolution provisions

4. **Provide Recommendations**
   - Suggest specific redlines
   - Explain business implications
   - Prioritize issues (deal-breakers vs. nice-to-haves)
   - Provide alternative language

### Case Analysis Workflow

When analyzing a case:

1. **Extract Key Information**
   - Parties and their roles
   - Procedural posture
   - Facts (material and background)
   - Legal issues presented
   - Holding and rationale
   - Dissents or concurrences (if relevant)

2. **Analyze the Reasoning**
   - What rule did the court apply?
   - How did the court apply facts to law?
   - What policy considerations influenced the decision?
   - What is the scope of the holding?

3. **Assess Applicability**
   - How does this case relate to the current matter?
   - Is it binding or persuasive authority?
   - Are the facts analogous or distinguishable?
   - Has this case been followed, limited, or overruled?

4. **Document for Use**
   - Create a case brief
   - Note useful quotes for citation
   - Identify related cases cited
   - Suggest how to use in argument

## Legal Analysis Frameworks

### IRAC Method
Use for organizing legal analysis:

**I**ssue: What is the legal question?
**R**ule: What is the applicable law?
**A**pplication: How does the law apply to these facts?
**C**onclusion: What is the result?

### CRAC Method
Alternative framework:

**C**onclusion: State the answer upfront
**R**ule: Explain the legal rule
**A**pplication: Apply rule to facts
**C**onclusion: Restate the result

### Statutory Interpretation
When interpreting statutes:

1. **Text**: Start with plain language
2. **Context**: Consider statutory structure and related provisions
3. **Purpose**: Consider legislative intent and purpose
4. **Canons**: Apply interpretive canons (e.g., ejusdem generis, expressio unius)
5. **Legislative History**: Review when text is ambiguous
6. **Precedent**: Consider how courts have interpreted the statute

## Specialized Practice Areas

### Contract Drafting Best Practices

**Clarity Principles:**
- Use "shall" for obligations, "may" for discretionary rights, "will" for future events
- Avoid "and/or" (use "or" or "and")
- Use active voice
- Define technical or ambiguous terms
- Use consistent terminology throughout

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Include force majeure clauses
- Define what constitutes material breach
- Specify remedies (liquidated damages, specific performance)
- Include survival clauses for key provisions
- Address confidentiality and data protection

### Discovery Management

**Document Review Approach:**
- Categorize by relevance, privilege, responsiveness
- Track privilege claims with privilege log
- Note custodian and source
- Flag hot documents requiring immediate attention
- Maintain review consistency with guidelines

**Interrogatory Response Strategy:**
- Object specifically and appropriately
- Respond subject to objections
- Be truthful but strategically concise
- Supplement if required by rules
- Coordinate with discovery plan

## Special Considerations

### Privilege and Confidentiality

When handling potentially privileged materials:
- Mark attorney work product appropriately
- Identify attorney-client communications
- Note privilege assertions in privilege log format
- Be cautious with disclosure to third parties
- Consider waiver implications

### Conflicts Checking

When analyzing new matters:
- Identify all parties and related entities
- Check for prior adverse representation
- Note potential positional conflicts
- Flag same-matter representation issues
- Consider imputation within firm

### Deadline Management

For time-sensitive matters:
- Calculate deadline using applicable rules (calendar days, business days, Rule 6)
- Account for holidays and weekends
- Note service method impact on deadlines
- Build in cushion for review and filing
- Flag statute of limitations issues

### Multi-Jurisdiction Issues

When dealing with multiple jurisdictions:
- Identify choice of law provisions
- Apply conflicts of law rules
- Note forum selection clauses
- Consider enforceability across jurisdictions
- Flag compliance requirements in each jurisdiction

## Quality Control

Before completing any legal work:

1. **Accuracy Check**
   - Verify all citations
   - Check party names and spelling
   - Confirm dates and numbers
   - Review calculations

2. **Completeness Check**
   - All required sections included
   - All exhibits referenced and attached
   - All authorities cited in argument appear in TOA
   - Certificate of service if required

3. **Consistency Check**
   - Defined terms used consistently
   - Cross-references accurate
   - Formatting uniform
   - Numbering sequential

4. **Compliance Check**
   - Court rules followed (page limits, margins, font)
   - Ethical rules satisfied
   - Filing requirements met
   - Signature requirements satisfied

## Limitations and Human Review Required

This skill assists with legal work but does NOT replace lawyer judgment. A licensed attorney must:

- Verify all legal research and citations
- Make final strategic decisions
- Ensure compliance with ethical obligations
- Sign all court filings and official documents
- Provide legal advice to clients
- Exercise professional judgment on all matters

**Always flag for attorney review:**
- Novel legal questions
- Conflicting authorities
- Ethical issues
- Statute of limitations concerns
- Privilege questions
- Settlement recommendations
- Case strategy decisions

## Tips for Effective Use

1. **Be Specific**: Provide jurisdiction, practice area, and specific facts
2. **Provide Context**: Share relevant background, documents, or prior research
3. **Iterate**: Start broad, then refine with follow-up questions
4. **Request Formats**: Specify if you want a memo, brief, bullet points, or table
5. **Flag Priorities**: Indicate what matters most (speed vs. thoroughness, key issues)
6. **Share Constraints**: Note page limits, deadlines, or other requirements
7. **Ask for Options**: Request alternatives when strategic decisions are involved
